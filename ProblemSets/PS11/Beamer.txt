\begin{frame}{Introduction}
\begin{itemize}
\item Using financial and stock market data for investment purposes.
\item Motivation: Gain insights into potential investments using new skills in data science.
\item Objective: Cut through market noise to find investments aligned with personal interests, such as dividend-paying stocks with low risk and growth potential.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Literature Review}
\begin{itemize}
\item Stability of dividend stocks: Consistent returns incentivize long-term investment, reducing volatility.
\item Investor preferences: Dividend stocks attract retail investors, particularly older and low-income investors, leading to longer holding periods.
\item Market influences on dividend decisions: Market demand and sentiment influence firms' decisions to pay dividends, impacting stock selection strategies.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Data and Methods}
\begin{itemize}
\item Data source: Initial dataset sourced from stockanalysis.com, containing information on Symbol, Dividend Yield, and Payout Ratio.
\item Filtering methods: Filtering for dividend yield, payout ratio, and historical consistency to refine the list of potential investments.
\item Methods for assessing historical consistency: Utilizing dividend payment history and financial data to select promising stock tickers.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Findings and Conclusion}
\begin{itemize}
\item Findings: Identification of 18 promising stock tickers meeting criteria for healthy dividend yields, consistent frequency, and positive historical profits.
\item Conclusion: Summary of project objectives, methods, and findings. Emphasis on informed decision-making in building an investment portfolio aligned with long-term financial goals.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
